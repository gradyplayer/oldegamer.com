Welcome to oldegamer.com

The purpose of this website is to document my study of the evolution of video games from the inception of hardware based o-scope/vectorscope games (like Tennis for Two) and mainframe based text games (like Lunar Lander, The Oregon Trail, and zork) in a rough chronological order through to purpose built TV games (like Pong), mostly skipping the first Generation of home consoles; picking up with the second generation of consoles like the Atari VCS/2600, Third Generation NES/Famicom, and picking up some early PC stuff, like IBM PS/2 era (VGA MODE!) and Atari 800, maybe Apple II... through the evolution to current technologies with engines like Unity and Unreal, and mobile gaming's infancy (maybe some Cocos2d).

All in all I expect this to be a major time-suck, and a lot of fun.

The initial incarnation of this site will be hand coded archeic html, so if your browser doesn't support <blink> you might be missing out on some of the fun.
